1. Abstract

Creating a website or an application is the the greatest thing ever done. You have no way of knowing if he will be successful or not, but the work is the same. Whether it's something complex or something simpler, any site or application needs a user guide. The next section describes BGT along with its components.


2. Introduction

The application represents a web system that gives the users the possibility to be for a couple of minutes visitors of a botanical garden. We give everyone who is a visitor of our website the chance to have access to all the information without any costs. To take the virtual tour the user has just toto navigate the page with the same name. Beside this, the visitors have the opportunity to make a difference by adopting a tree and making a donation.


3. Structure

The application contains 8 pages: Home, About, Tour Dates, Presentations, Virtual Tour, History, Contact and Adopt a Tree. The content of each page can be found in the following paragraphs.

3.1. Home

Home is the fisrt interaction of the user with the website. Here, the visitor can fnd the main menu in the left side of the screen and in the center, the visitor can see unfolding some images representing attractions of the botanical garden.

3.2. About

In here, the visitor can see a video presentation of the botanical garden at the top of the page. He also has some additional information on the middle of the page and he laso has access to the links to social media networks.

3.3. Tour Dates

This page consists information regarding to the future dates when virtual tours will be organized together with a specialized guide. The visitors can also follow to see more interesting things on social media. Also new rules about the covid-19 virus and how the activity will be carried out taking into account the new situation.

3.4. Presentations

On this page the user is able to see a gallery list of the main of the main attractions of the botanical garden. In order to find out more information about the exhibits, the user has to click on the picture representing its element of iterest.

3.5. Virtual Tour

Being a site that does not require a prepayment to perform a virtual tour, visitors can do this by simply accessing this page. Visitors can take a virtual tour by viewing the most important attractions of the garden.

3.6. History

Here, the visitors can find out more information about the history of the botanical garden.

3.7. Contact

Contact page gives the visitor information about the persons that can be reached in case of a critical situation or just to inform something. It also has the location, phone number and email addres of the botanical garden. The social media links are also available in case the visitor wants to see more of the activity developed.

3.8. Adopt a Tree

Trying to do the world a better place, the visitor has the possibility to adopt a tree. On the Adopt a Tree page, he has to fill in a form using the appropriate information. After filling in all the required information, the user has to click on the Submit button. He also has the chance to make a donation to help to the maintenance of the botanical garden. This can be like a protest to stop cutting down trees around the globe.
